  • Martial Arts - Taekwondo

    Martial Arts - Taekwondo

    I attended Yun’s Black Belt Academy earning my Cho Dan Bo first probationary black belt in 1990 which allowed me to train for the next six months and prove my worthiness as a first degree black belt. I later inherited the Academy from my Master instructor and re-organized it as Horizon Martial Arts Academy.

    Horizon Martial Arts Academy President CEO and Master Instructor The Academy prospered and grew over the next 18 years into 121 full time martial arts students ranging in rank from White to 2nd Black belts. Studnets ages of 3 to 63. With over 22 certified Black Belts and 12 Certified 2nd degree black Instructors. located in Oshkosh and Omro Wisconsin. Horizon Martial Arts Officiation included recognition with the World Taekwondo Federation, United Taekwondo do Federation, International Taekwondo federation and the Hapkido federation. The practice of the art of Taekwondo was traditional focusing Taekwondo Chonji style emphasizing all the aspects of Korean taekwondo, Weapons, Hapkido and Yudo not to be confused with Judo. I semi-retired from Horizon Martial Arts Academy in 2002, Where I continued to assist the black belt instructors in training and testing. I continued my journey in the martial arts from this point forward with HaeSan Martial arts where I and my Black belts students where adopted and continued advanced training in the traditional art of Taekwondo learning the art, history, Korean language and sharing it with our students.

    Technology

    In the late 80’s I was introduced to the world of technology I found many needs for computers and technology. I was intrigued and curious as to how it all worked from a fundamental need to know. I taught myself all of the basics. Learned the ins and outs of a variety of software application. I still had the need for more. I obtained a contract position with Oshkosh Truck as a desktop publisher which morphed into a 2 year contract and promotion to the lead desktop graphic design publisher.

    Technology was exploding, almost a third of the homes in the United states had a PC. Times were great I was building, troubleshooting and networking PC’s. I could not get enough. I started dabbling in all of the above while working at and learning from other techy types…there were no formal programs as that time so the sky was the limit if you could hook up with a store that sold computers you could play with all the technology from the build, troubleshooting, supporting and the networking times where good I had an equal balance of Martial arts and Technology. Then it happened the introduction to the Internet…. thanks to Al Gore ;o) Ha…..I still find that funny. In any case the internet was here. The rest is history. I was obsessed with technology; I could not get enough of it. The more I explored the more I learned, the more I wanted. It was all fresh and new. Everything you needed to know or wanted to know was as the tips of your fingers The internet was the new form of information. I was hooked.
